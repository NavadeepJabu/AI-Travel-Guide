**Essential Travel Tips for Paris, France**

**🏛️ Local Customs & Etiquette:**
* **Greeting Etiquette** - Always greet shopkeepers and restaurant staff with "Bonjour" (good day) or "Bonsoir" (good evening). It's considered rude to start a conversation without a proper greeting.
* **Dining Etiquette** - Keep your hands on the table (not in your lap) during meals. Tipping is not obligatory as service charge is included, but leaving 5-10% for excellent service is appreciated.
* **Dress Code** - Parisians tend to dress more formally. Avoid overly casual attire like athletic wear when visiting restaurants or cultural sites.

**🚗 Transportation Guide:**
* **Metro System** - The Paris Metro is efficient and extensive. Buy a Navigo weekly pass (Monday-Sunday) or book of 10 tickets for better value. Single tickets cost €1.90.
* **Walking** - Paris is very walkable. Central attractions are often within 15-20 minutes of each other. Wear comfortable shoes!
* **Ride-sharing** - Uber and Bolt operate in Paris, but can be expensive during peak hours. Official taxis are readily available at designated stands.

**🛡️ Safety & Security:**
* **Pickpocketing** - Be especially vigilant in tourist areas, on the Metro, and near major attractions. Keep valuables in front pockets or secure bags.
* **Scams** - Be wary of petition signers and bracelet sellers near tourist sites. Politely decline and walk away.
* **Emergency Numbers** - European emergency number is 112. Police specific number is 17.

**📅 Best Time to Visit:**
* **April to June** - Ideal spring weather with blooming flowers, fewer crowds than summer, and pleasant temperatures (15-20°C).
* **September to October** - Beautiful autumn colors, comfortable weather, and reduced summer crowds. Perfect for museum visits and outdoor dining.
* **July to August** - Peak tourist season with hot weather (25-30°C) and largest crowds. Many locals go on vacation in August.

**💡 Local Insights & Useful Advice:**
* **Museum Pass** - Consider the Paris Museum Pass for skip-the-line access and savings if visiting multiple museums. 4-day pass costs €70.
* **Dining Times** - Lunch is typically 12:00-14:00, dinner 19:30-22:00. Restaurants often close between these hours.
* **Shopping Hours** - Most shops close around 19:00, and many smaller shops close on Sundays. Department stores on Champs-Élysées stay open later.
* **Free Water** - Tap water is safe to drink in Paris. Carry a reusable bottle and refill at public fountains.
* **Bakeries** - Visit local boulangeries in the morning for the freshest croissants and bread. Many sell out by afternoon!

**Additional Tips:**
* Learn basic French phrases - "Merci" (thank you), "S'il vous plaît" (please), and "Excusez-moi" (excuse me) go a long way.
* Many museums are free for EU residents under 26 and for everyone on first Sunday of each month.
* The Paris City Hall (Hôtel de Ville) offers free walking tours and exhibitions.
* Consider staying in neighborhoods like Le Marais or Saint-Germain for authentic local experiences.
